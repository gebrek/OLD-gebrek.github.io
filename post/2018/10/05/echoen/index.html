Echoen

Jacob Sonnenberg

2018/10/05

Tags: prosetry

Life's full of do's and don't's. That many apostrophes may be among them.

How to catch cache the fleeting thoughts they whisk by like lost time. Who's the elephant who's the rider. How much of now is back then? How have previous potentials reduced to now? Where in time do my roots belong?

There are sleeves of me sloughed off and withered from memory, but existing as standing waves in time. Narrow tired eyes cannot recall infinite iterations.

Too busy to edit better. Too busy to notice time float by on clouds of smoke. Just the present vision, prophecy of now. Until torn in twain by unbearable pain. Until then is born again into the new millennium.

Sedimentary shift, an archaeological marker of a transition. A tree's ring speaking of winter and summer and empire's fall. Now is forever but we leave it behind. A lake dimpled by rain's drop over aion becomes mountain tops. Yet we fly through the eternal present as the dawn rises on each new day. Stationary as statues. Bursting forward to meet the future but being dashed against inescapable finitude.
